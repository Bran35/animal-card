Cane Corso's

Corso is an ancient breed.

The Corso's lineage goes back to ancient Roman times,

and the breed's name roughly translates from the Latin as 'bodyguard dog.

  • Number: #340
  • Type: italian Mastiff
  • Abilities: Protective nature
  • Evolutions:Roman war dogs

At 28 inches at the shoulder and often weighing more than 100 pounds, with a large head,

and muscles rippling beneath their short, stiff coat, Corsi are at a glance intimidating creatures.

Their imposing appearance is their first line of defense against intruders.
